Multisyllabic rhyme

Every caught a bar so clever, you had to rewind? It's not magic - probably a mutisyballic rhyme!

A multisyllabic rhyme (also called polysyllabic rhyme) is when two or more syllables in a word or phrase rhyme with two or more syllables in another word or phrase.

Think of multisyllabic rhymes like choreographed footwork in a danceâ€”the more intricate the steps, the smoother the flow. Instead of just rhyming single words, artists stack entire syllables, creating patterns that lock into the beat like puzzle pieces.

This technique makes rap lyrics sound more complex and engaging.

The rhymes can sometimes mirror the beat, making the lyrics sound more fluid. Multisyllabic rhymes add complexity to lyrics, and let's be honest, they are more impressive.
